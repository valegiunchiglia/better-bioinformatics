Dr Naveed Ishaque

"Making sense of cells in space: challenges in interpreting spatial transcriptomics data"

Recommended reading

  • Metrics reloaded A review of how metrics should be used and interpreted. Not covered directly in the talk, but well worth reading.
  • Beyond benchmarking Naveed's group on the issues with methods, data, ground truths, and metrics when benchmarking spatial transcriptomics tools. Due to be peer-reviewed soon.
  • Challenges and prospects for cell segmentation in spatial transcriptomics A perspectives piece on the current landscape of tools and open problems, and where the field is heading. Part of the Human Cell Atlas.

Tools mentioned

  • SSAM An early cell-segmentation-free analysis tool that generates cell-type maps. Not fast enough for current technologies.
  • Sainsc An improved, faster cell-segmentation-free analysis tool from the same group.
  • FICTURE Another cell-segmentation-free tool, focused on gene expression programs and factors rather than cell typing.
  • ovrl.py Detects cell overlap signals (vertical doublets) in spatial transcriptomics data.
  • CellAdMix Identifies and corrects transcript misassignment.
  • SPLIT Improves transcript assignment to cells by resolving mixed signals.
  • SpatialLeiden The spatial extension of the popular Leiden algorithm. Familiar and fast.
  • CellCharter A high-performing spatial clustering tool.
