A seminar series about the why behind bioinformatics, not just the how.

Better Bioinformatics is an independent, community-run seminar for computational biology, bioinformatics, and AI/ML in the life sciences. Every session digs into the reasoning behind an analytical choice, so the work behind the results is rigorous, robust, and reproducible.

What to expect

Practical, honest talks — not polished research pitches.

Each session features one guest speaker from industry or academia, talking about a method or challenge they know inside out.

🎤

Real experts, real experience

Speakers come from across academia and industry — the people actually building and using the tools you rely on.

🔍

Reasoning, not just recipes

We ask speakers to explain the thinking behind an analytical choice, common pitfalls, and how to avoid them — not just which command to run.

💬

Room for real questions

Every talk ends with open Q&A, because the best insights usually come out in the discussion afterwards.

How a session runs

The format, every time.

We ask every speaker to follow roughly the same structure, so the series stays focused on why an approach makes sense rather than a tour of a toolkit.

Talks run for around 50 minutes, followed by 10 minutes of open questions.

1

Background

A quick primer on the topic, so everyone starts from the same place.

2

What

A common pitfall — what people usually get wrong.

3

Why

Why the mistake matters, and how it can quietly change your results.

4

How

Practical guidance for doing it right, and the principles to follow instead.

5

Q&A

Ten minutes of open questions — bring the thing you've always wondered about.

Who it's for

Built for people who already know their way around the basics.

The content is pitched at an intermediate audience — mostly PhD students and postdocs who already have some familiarity with bioinformatics or computational biology concepts, and basic coding skills. Come with questions.
